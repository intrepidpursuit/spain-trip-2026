Beach Day — Cantarrijan
One of the best naturist beaches in Andalusia, inside the Maro-Cerro Gordo Natural Park near Almuñécar. Dramatic cliff scenery, crystal water ideal for snorkeling, divided into textile and naturist sections each with a beach bar. Mixed crowd, all ages, good energy.

Seville — 2 nights
The Alcázar palace is one of the most extraordinary buildings in the world — book tickets in advance. The Gothic cathedral next door is vast and unmissable. Evenings: the Triana neighborhood for tapas and authentic flamenco (not the tourist show). Check the city tourism site for smaller local flamenco venues.

Granada — 1 night
The Alhambra needs at least half a day — book tickets weeks in advance, they sell out. Every bar serves free tapas with drinks. Stay in the Albaicín neighborhood for views across to the Alhambra at night.
Morocco decision point: From Granada, Tarifa is 2hrs south and the Tangier ferry is 45 minutes. If Morocco is happening, it starts from here tomorrow morning.

Tarifa → Ferry → Tangier
The FRS ferry from Tarifa is 45 minutes. Tangier: the old medina, the Kasbah on the hill, views back toward Spain. Matisse and Delacroix painted here; Paul Bowles lived here for decades. Stay in a riad — a traditional courtyard townhouse, plain outside, stunning within.

Sep 21
Chefchaouen — The Blue City
Two hours inland through the Rif Mountains. The entire old city is painted in shades of blue by Moorish and Jewish refugees expelled from Spain in 1492. The Spanish Mosque above town has panoramic views. Tagines, couscous, fresh bread with honey. The kind of place you wander rather than check things off. Side trip option: Volubilis — Morocco's best-preserved Roman ruins, 1st century AD mosaics and arches still standing in open landscape.

Playa Illa Roja near Begur — internationally known naturist beach in a dramatic cove, accessed through a wooded path, peaceful and relatively uncrowded. Or Cala Sa Boadella in Lloret de Mar — hidden naturist cove behind a pine forest, Costa Brava blue water. Either is a great slow beach day.

🏖 Beach Day
Barcelona — Mar Bella
Mar Bella is Barcelona's clothing-optional beach — explicitly known for attracting a young, cosmopolitan crowd: students, locals, water sports enthusiasts. LGBTQ-friendly, lively, 15 minutes from city center in the Poblenou district. The one most likely to have the mix you're looking for.
Also great: Sitges — 30 min by train, LGBTQ-friendly beach town with three clothing-optional beaches and a younger, lively crowd. Great as a day trip or overnight.
