Spain & Maybe Beyond

Pulled together by Claude based on your interests and the wedding details

Your anniversary is September 15th and the wedding is the 26th, so you'd likely be in Europe for both. Here's how to think about shaping the trip.

The Anchor

Girona, Catalonia

The wedding is at Mas Rosset — a beautiful estate outside Girona. Girona itself is worth several days: medieval walls, a stunning cathedral, a well-preserved Jewish quarter, and a genuinely local feel. Only 40 minutes by train from Barcelona. Late September is ideal — warm, uncrowded, past the summer tourist surge.

While You're There

Catalonia & the Costa Brava

Dalí Theatre-Museum — Figueres

Dalí designed the entire building as a work of art. One of the stranger and more wonderful museums in Europe — not a typical gallery at all. About 45 minutes from Girona.

ArtMuseum45 min away
Cadaqués

A whitewashed fishing village on the Costa Brava that feels completely off the tourist trail. Rugged coastline, great seafood, very slow pace. Dalí lived here — the house is now a museum.

El Fonoll — Naturist Village

This one is on the list for him, but worth considering together. A restored medieval village — ancient stone buildings, organic gardens, a pool, communal Sunday paellas. Clothing is optional, not required. A genuinely unique historic place most tourists never find.

Option A · Extend South

Andalusia

Adding 4–5 days south opens up some of Spain's most spectacular historic sites. The Moorish history here is extraordinary — this region was under Islamic rule for nearly 800 years and the architecture reflects it at every turn.

Seville

The Alcázar palace is Moorish architecture at its peak — a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Also home to authentic flamenco, not the tourist-show version.

ArchitectureFlamencoUNESCO
Granada

The Alhambra is genuinely unmissable — one of the most extraordinary palace complexes in the world. Bars here still serve free tapas with every drink.

Ruins & PalacesFoodUNESCO
Córdoba

The Mezquita — a mosque-cathedral hybrid that shouldn't architecturally work but absolutely does. One of the most unusual and beautiful buildings in Europe.

ArchitectureHistoryUNESCO
Option B · Extend North

Basque Country

A completely different vibe — cooler, greener, and extraordinarily food-obsessed. The Basques have a distinct culture, language, and identity that makes this feel like a different country entirely.

San Sebastián

One of the great food cities of the world. More Michelin stars per capita than almost anywhere on earth. The pintxos bar scene is incredible and deeply local — and could make for a very special anniversary dinner on the 15th.

FoodAnniversaryCoastal
Bilbao — Guggenheim

The Guggenheim here is world-class, and Frank Gehry's titanium-clad building is as impressive as what's inside. The surrounding waterfront has been completely transformed by its presence.

ArtArchitectureMuseum
Option C · Cross a Continent

Morocco

This one deserves serious consideration. A 45-minute ferry from Tarifa in southern Spain drops you into North Africa — a completely different world in terms of culture, architecture, food, and sensory experience. It pairs naturally with the Andalusia option. Two to four days is enough to get a real taste without rushing.

Getting there: The ferry runs from Tarifa to Tangier — about 45 minutes. Well-organized private guided tours that handle everything (ferry, transport, local guides, riads) are very well-reviewed and recommended for a first visit.
Tangier

More interesting than its reputation suggests. Sits at the crossroads of the Mediterranean and Atlantic, where Europe meets Africa. Matisse, Delacroix, and Paul Bowles all lived or worked here. The old medina, Kasbah, and views back toward Spain make it a fascinating half-day.

HistoryArchitectureFood
Chefchaouen — The Blue City

Two hours inland through the Rif Mountains. The entire old city is painted in shades of blue — a tradition dating to Moorish and Jewish refugees expelled from Spain in 1492. The Spanish Mosque above town has panoramic views; the central square has a kasbah and open-air cafes. The food is outstanding. The kind of place you wander rather than check off a list.

ArchitectureHistoryFoodUnmissable
Volubilis — Roman Ruins

The best-preserved Roman ruins in Morocco — 1st century AD, with intact mosaic floors, triumphal arches, and columns still standing in an open landscape. Rewards slow exploration. Pairs well with the nearby hilltown of Moulay Idriss.

RuinsRomanHistory
Tétouan

A less-visited alternative to Chefchaouen, closer to Tangier. Its medina is a UNESCO World Heritage Site with strong Hispano-Moorish character. Souks here are more local and less tourist-oriented.

ArchitectureUNESCOOff the Beaten Path
Where to stay: Stay in a riad — a traditional townhouse built around a central courtyard, often with a rooftop terrace. Plain on the outside, typically stunning within. One of those experiences genuinely unique to this part of the world.
Option D · Further Afield

Portugal or Southern France

You've already done London, Paris, Amsterdam, and the Cotswolds. A couple of easy additions that pair naturally with Spain:

Porto, Portugal

Inexpensive to reach from Spain, architecturally stunning, and much less visited than Lisbon. The wine culture is wonderful and the food is excellent. A gritty, lived-in beauty that rewards slow exploration.

ArchitectureFood & WineOff the Beaten Path
Carcassonne, Southern France

Only about 2 hours from Girona — a medieval fortified city that's spectacular and an easy stop en route. One of the best-preserved medieval citadels in Europe.

Practical Notes

The Dogs & Overall Shape

The dogs: Delta doesn't allow pets in cabin on international flights. Options are boarding them for the trip or researching other airlines. Worth sorting out early since it shapes everything else.

Trip length: Based on a slower pace — one or two things a day, good meals, time to wander — somewhere in the range of 12–16 days makes sense depending on how far you extend.

Routing: Fly into Barcelona, work your way around, fly home from a second city to avoid backtracking.
